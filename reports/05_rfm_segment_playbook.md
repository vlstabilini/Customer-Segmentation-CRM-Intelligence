## 🥇 1. Champions (1,121 customers · 65.9% revenue)

- **Who:** Recent buyers, high frequency, high value. The heart of the business.
- **Why they matter:** Two-thirds of all revenue. Losing 100 = losing £500k+.
- **Action:** VIP program · early access · exclusive content · referral incentives
- **Channel:** Personalized email + account-manager touch for top-tier
- **Budget:** Highest LTV-weighted retention spend
- **KPI:** Retention rate · NPS · referral conversion
- **Avoid:** Discounting (you train them to wait); over-emailing

---

## 🥈 2. Loyal Customers (329 customers · 9.1% revenue)

- **Who:** Frequent buyers but slightly less recent than Champions.
- **Why they matter:** One tier removed from Champions — promotion candidates.
- **Action:** Upsell · cross-sell · loyalty tier upgrade incentives
- **Channel:** Triggered email · personalized product recommendations
- **Budget:** High; ROI is strong on upgrade campaigns
- **KPI:** Migration rate to Champions · basket value
- **Avoid:** Treating them like a generic email list

---

## 🌱 3. Potential Loyalists (304 customers · 4.6% revenue)

- **Who:** Recent buyers with moderate frequency — primed to climb.
- **Why they matter:** Highest **upside per customer** of any mid-tier segment.
- **Action:** Onboarding completion · 2nd-product cross-sell · category education
- **Channel:** Lifecycle email sequences
- **Budget:** Medium-high; payoff is in frequency uplift
- **KPI:** Frequency uplift in next 90 days
- **Avoid:** Overwhelming them with a full product catalog before they're ready

---

## ✨ 4. New Customers (99 customers · 0.6% revenue) ⭐ Highest leverage

- **Who:** Just bought. Within the 30-day window.
- **Why they matter:** Phase 4 H3 finding — fast 2nd-purchasers have **11.9× the CLV** of one-timers.
- **Action:** **30-day post-purchase nurture** · welcome series · product education · 2nd-purchase trigger at day 25
- **Channel:** Automated email · in-app onboarding
- **Budget:** Medium per-customer; **biggest CRM ROI lever** in the project
- **KPI:** 2nd-purchase rate within 30 days
- **Avoid:** Waiting. The window closes fast.

---

## 🌿 5. Promising (211 customers · 1.0% revenue)

- **Who:** Recent but not yet frequent. Could go either way.
- **Why they matter:** Low cost to nudge, decent upside.
- **Action:** 2nd-purchase incentive · gentle category-broadening campaign
- **Channel:** Email + light retargeting
- **Budget:** Medium
- **KPI:** 2nd-purchase rate

---

## 😴 6. About to Sleep (351 customers · 1.8% revenue)

- **Who:** Recency slipping — they're cooling off but not yet at-risk.
- **Why they matter:** Cheap to reactivate **before** they fully churn.
- **Action:** Re-engagement email · "we miss you" · limited-time offer
- **Channel:** Email · light retargeting
- **Budget:** Low-medium
- **KPI:** Open rate · reactivation rate

---

## ⚠️ 7. At Risk (885 customers · 6.8% revenue)

- **Who:** Used to buy with some frequency. Now silent.
- **Why they matter:** **Largest recoverable pool by count.** Combined with Cannot Lose Them, drives Hypothesis H5.
- **Action:** Structured win-back campaign · personalized discount · feedback survey to diagnose churn cause
- **Channel:** Email · SMS · direct outreach for high-monetary tail
- **Budget:** High per-customer (justified by past value)
- **KPI:** Reactivation rate · recovered revenue
- **Avoid:** Generic re-engagement language

---

## 🚨 8. Cannot Lose Them (285 customers · 5.1% revenue) ⭐ Urgent action

- **Who:** Proven loyal (high frequency) but currently dormant. Average historical value £1,590 each.
- **Why they matter:** **Highest cost of loss per customer** in the entire taxonomy. Each lost customer = multi-£1,000 revenue event.
- **Action:** **Account-manager outreach** (top tier) · custom offer · root-cause survey
- **Channel:** Direct human contact for top half · personalized email for rest
- **Budget:** **Highest per-customer spend justified** — cost of loss is real and quantified
- **KPI:** Recovery rate (binary: did they return within 60 days?)
- **Avoid:** Putting them in a bulk win-back funnel. They've earned individualized attention.

---

## 💤 9. Hibernating (753 customers · 5.2% revenue)

- **Who:** Lowest recency, lowest frequency, lowest value.
- **Why they matter:** Mostly *don't* matter — but as a group they cost real money if you over-invest.
- **Action:** Minimal — annual reactivation campaign only
- **Channel:** Bulk email (very low frequency)
- **Budget:** **Lowest** — explicitly *do not over-invest*
- **KPI:** Suppression list health · cost per reactivated customer
- **Avoid:** Treating them like At Risk. Different segment, different priority.

---

## ✅ Hypothesis H5 — Verdict

| Hypothesis | Verdict | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| H5: Dormant-but-previously-loyal customers are recoverable with targeted win-back | ✅ **CONFIRMED** | 1,170 customers in At Risk + Cannot Lose Them segments represent **£1.06M (11.9%)** of historical revenue. Even 20% recovery rate = £210k recovered. |
